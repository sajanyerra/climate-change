# 🌍 Against Climate Change
### A Power BI Data Storytelling Project — University of Limerick

---

> **⚠️ Disclaimer:** The arguments presented in this project are intentionally
> constructed and do not reflect the scientific consensus on climate change.
> This was an academic exercise in understanding how data can be framed
> to support biased narratives.

---

## 📌 About This Project

This project was completed as part of **MI6201 – Data and Analytical Decision Making**
at the **Kemmy Business School, University of Limerick**.

The core objective was **not** to deny climate change — but to demonstrate something
arguably more important: **how data can be selectively presented to construct
convincing but misleading arguments.**

By taking real, reputable datasets and framing them through a sceptical lens,
we built a Power BI report that appears to argue climate change is overstated.
The exercise sharpens critical thinking around:

- 📊 How visualisations can mislead
- 🔍 The difference between correlation and causation
- ✂️ The danger of cherry-picking data
- 🧠 How to identify bias in data narratives

## 🔍 Topics Covered

- 🌡️ **Temperature Deviation (1850–2022)** — Global anomaly trends vs. Paris Agreement targets
- 🏭 **Greenhouse Gas Emissions** — CO₂, CH₄, and N₂O trends over 170 years
- 🇰🇷 **South Korea Case Study** — Regional GHG and temperature patterns
- 🌧️ **Irish Precipitation Patterns** — Local rainfall data from 1960–2020
- 🌋 **Volcanic Eruptions & ENSO** — Natural climate drivers and their impact
- 🧊 **Greenland Ice Sheet Data** — Historical temperature variability over millennia
- 🌿 **Positive Effects of CO₂** — Crop yields, plant growth, and cold-related mortality

> essential skill for analysts, decision-makers, and informed citizens alike.
